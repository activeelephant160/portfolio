Part 1: Finding Usability Problems

Picking a Webpage

This link displays one of Mumbai’s most famous apartment building’s website. The video below displays the landing page of the website. The building spans across 7 acres, has three high-rise towers and has won several awards for it’s design, management and architecture. Despite the accolades, its website is archaic with poor usability. The purpose of this website is to attract prospective residents (buyers or renters), and right now it does a poor job at displaying the benefits of living in this apartment. It doesn’t convey competitive advantages, amenities, location or apartment options in a cohesive, emphatic manner which could put off potential buyers / renters from the building.

Finding Problems

Learnability & Efficiency

Task Learnability: Can users learn how to use this website easily? Efficiency: How fast can users perform tasks once they learn to use the website?
Check Available Apartments Navbar has options to check out apartments for rent / sale; Lengthy wording with repetitive mention of “BeauMonde Towers” Efficient as shortcut is available on the Navbar however it is listed repetitively as under rent and purchase
Check Amenities Amenties are on the landing page but it is hard to find unless the user reads the entire page. Amenties are written one below the other without distinctive visual aid or proper listing order (1., 2. etc) which makes it hard to locate Amenties are not distincty listed on the homepage and there isn't any text difference, visual aid, so even if users know where it is - they can’t scan the page to find it - they would have to read the entire page
General Information Home page offers general information in a vague manner as there is no distinct, succint paragraph describing what BeauMonde Towers really is General Information is not distincty listed on the homepage and there isn't any text difference, visual aid, so even if users know where it is - they can’t scan the page to find it - they would have to read the entire page
Information about Location Although a map icon is available for location, the section is located at the bottom of the page with no quick shortcut method to reach there (nothing in the navbar). Located at the bottom of the page with no quick shortcut method to reach there. If users know to look for it at the bottom it would be efficient but there’s no shortcut or map icon to make it more efficient.
Apartment Options / Sizes Apartment sizes are available but only on the homepage at the bottom; no shortcut available and there isn't any visual aid to support it - only text Information about apartments is not distincty listed on the homepage and there isn't any text difference, visual aid, so even if users know where it is - they can’t scan the page to find it - they would have to read the entire pag
Contact Management Contact us shortcut in the menu available; fairly intuitive Efficient as shortcut as availble on the menu

Memorability: How well can a user remember different functions after they have learned the function?

I feel like overall if the user is familiar with the website and knows what their looking for, the website is memorable enough to use. Since there isn’t too much functionality except looking for information and contacting the management - the website achieves that function. There are definitely ways to make the website more memorable and easier to use but it achieves that functionality right now

Satisfaction: How pleasing is it to use?

  • There is too much information on the homepage and it hasn’t been organized effectively.
    • Amenties (a huge part of the apartment building) have been listed in an ineffective manner with no distinction between Amenties and other information about the building
    • The same goes for all other information - overview, apartment sizes, location etc
  • The only mode of information exchanged is through text - there are very few icons, images, graphics - which makes it inaccessible and unneccarily wordy (violating what Cooper said in About Face: The Essentials of Interaction Design, “Perfection is achieved not when there is no longer anything to add, but when there is no longer anything to take away.")
  • There’s too much repetive information in the Title, Subtitle and Menu bar (too many mentions of “BeauMonde Towers”) making unneccarily long
  • The blue border image is unrelated to the apartment building
  • The first image of the building is not focused on just the “towers”
  • Too many paragraphs and less bullets
  • Typography is not consistent and cluttered
  • Most hyperlinks are to Wikipedia pages and are not neccessary

Accessibility: WebAIM Wave

According to WebAIM WAVE the website has 4 errors, 2 contrast errors, 3 fields missing alternate text, 6 small texts and 6 redundant titles. I agree with this assessment as it seems the information is described ineffectively in alternate texts and could be more comprehensive.
